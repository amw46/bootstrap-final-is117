MAY 2018

Another day of filming of the Beatles second feature fim, "Help!" was occuring on this day in 1965 (May 4th). The Fab Four and the film crew were on location at Salisbury Plains in Wiltshire. It is very windy as the group mimes to George Harrison's track, "I Need You". Troops from a division of the Royal Artillery were also there doing exercises: they allowed their equipment to feature in the film.
On May 3rd, 1967, sessions for the song (not the album!) "Magical Mystery Tour" were nearly completed. They brought in a handful of musicians to overdub some trumpet-playing onto the song. Paul McCartney struggled to communicate what he wanted them to play. One of the trumpeters eventually got tired and played a score himself: this gets used on the final cut.
The Beatles record George Harrison's prized song, "Something", on May 2nd, 1969. The song was recognized by everybody at the session as an instant classic-- today, it is considered one of Harrison's best compositions. The release of "Something" as a single would be the first time that a Harrison-penned song would be the A-side.
On May 1st, 1966, The Beatles appear at their last scheduled concert appearance in the United Kingdom: the New Musical Express Annual Poll-Winners' All-Star Concert. The venue is the Empire Pool in Wembley, London. Other headliners include Herman's Hermits, Roy Orbison, and The Seekers. The Beatles play a fifteen-minute set to an audience of 10,000. Songs included "I Feel Fine", "Nowhere Man", "Day Tripper", "If I Needed Someone" and "I'm Down". At the end, they are presented poll awards.
