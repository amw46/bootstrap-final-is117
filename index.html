THE BEATLES. So much is in a name. The Fab Four from Liverpool have entertained audiences of all shapes, sizes, and generations since they hit the scene in 1962. John, Paul, George, and Ringo arguably changed the music scene forever with their ingenuity, quick wit, and instantly-recognizable songs.

This is a loving tribute to the Beatles and their music.

WITH A LIITLE HELP FROM THEIR FRIENDS

George Martin

1926-2016

Martin was the Beatles' producer, arranger and the head of Parlophone Records at EMI. Paul McCartney has referred to him as "the fifth Beatle": his work on Beatles records is extensive.

Brian Epstein

1934-1967

Epstein's family owned a large record shop in England. After a patron at his store requested a Beatles record, he tracked the foursome down at the Cavern Club. Epstein was instantly impressed by their performance. He cleaned up their stage act and became their manager until his death in 1967.

Yoko Ono

1933-

Ono is famously known as the second wife of John Lennon. They met at an art gallery in 1966 and were wed in 1969. They remained together until Lennon's death, and to this day she works to preserve his legacy.

Pete Best

1941-

From 1960 to 1962, Best was the first drummer of The Quarrymen, a skiffle group reduced to the trio of Lennon/Harrison/McCartney. The group, the newly-christened "Beatles", had been seeking a drummer to fill in an empty place during their Hamburg shows. Epstein had Best replaced with Ringo Starr.

Stuart Sutcliffe

1940-1962

Known as the original bass player for the Beatles, Sutcliffe was John Lennon's friend from art school. He was recruited to play bass guitar with The Silver Beatles/The Quarrymen in 1960. Sutcliffe left the group to pursue his love of painting. His departure from the group forced McCartney to learn the instrument.
